Poori Recipe

Poori

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 tablespoon semolina (optional, for crispness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon oil or ghee
  • Water, as needed for kneading
  • Oil, for deep frying

Instructions:

1. Preparing the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add the wheat flour, semolina (if using), and salt.
  2. Drizzle 1 teaspoon of oil or ghee over the flour.
  3. Add water little by little and knead the flour into a stiff dough. The dough should not be soft.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

2. Rolling the Pooris

  1. After resting, divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.
  2. Roll each ball into a small circle about 4-5 inches in diameter. Keep the thickness medium (not too thick or thin).
  3. Make sure to roll evenly so the pooris puff up well while frying.

3. Frying the Pooris

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
  2. Slide one poori gently into the hot oil. It should immediately start puffing up.
  3. Gently press the poori with a slotted spoon to help it puff up evenly.
  4. Fry the poori on both sides until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  6. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

4. Serving the Pooris

  1. Serve hot pooris with aloo sabzi, chana masala, or any curry of your choice.
