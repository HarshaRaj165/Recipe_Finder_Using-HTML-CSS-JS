Chapathi Recipe

Poori

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (optional)
  • Water, as needed for kneading
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking the chapathis

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, add the wheat flour and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of oil or ghee (optional) for softer chapathis.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, pliable dough. Knead well for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 20-30 minutes.

2. Rolling the Chapathis

  1. After resting, divide the dough into equal-sized balls (about the size of a lime).
  2. Roll each ball into a small, smooth circle using a rolling pin. Dust with flour to prevent sticking.
  3. Roll out evenly to about 6-7 inches in diameter. The chapathis should be thin but not too thin.

3. Cooking the Chapathis

  1. Heat a tawa or flat griddle over medium-high heat.
  2. Place the rolled chapathi on the hot tawa. Cook for a few seconds until small bubbles appear on the surface.
  3. Flip the chapathi and cook the other side. Press gently with a clean cloth or spatula to help it puff up.
  4. Optionally, apply a little oil or ghee to both sides while cooking for extra flavor.
  5. Once cooked, remove the chapathi from the tawa and keep it warm in a cloth or container.
  6. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

4. Serving the Chapathis

  1. Serve hot chapathis with any curry, dal, or yogurt.
