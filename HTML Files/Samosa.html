Samosa Recipe

Samosa

Ingredients:

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, for kneading

For the Filling

  • 3-4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil

For Frying

  • Oil, for deep frying

Instructions:

1. Preparing the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, salt, and carom seeds.
  2. Add oil or ghee and mix well with your fingers until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

2. Preparing the Filling

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add finely chopped green chilies and saut√© for a minute.
  3. Add boiled green peas, mashed potatoes, coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Turn off the heat and add chopped coriander leaves. Let the filling cool.

3. Shaping the Samosa

  1. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  2. Roll each ball into an oval-shaped sheet about 6 inches in diameter.
  3. Cut the rolled sheet into two halves to form two semicircles.
  4. Take one semicircle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edges using water.
  5. Fill the cone with the prepared potato-pea filling, then seal the open edge tightly by pressing it together with water.

4. Frying the Samosa

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat.
  2. Carefully place the samosas in the hot oil and fry them on medium heat until golden brown and crisp.
  3. Remove the samosas and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

5. Serving

  1. Serve hot samosas with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.
