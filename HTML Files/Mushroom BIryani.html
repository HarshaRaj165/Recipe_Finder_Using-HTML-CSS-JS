Mushroom Biryani Recipe

Mushroom Biryani

Ingredients:

For the Mushroom Marinade

  • 200g mushrooms (button or oyster, sliced)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt, to taste

For the Rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt, to taste

For the Biryani

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Marinating the Mushrooms

  1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, biryani masala, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Add the sliced mushrooms and toss gently to coat them in the marinade.
  3. Let it marinate for at least 20-30 minutes.

2. Preparing the Rice

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot. Add the bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Once the water comes to a boil, add the soaked basmati rice. Cook until the rice is about 70-80% done (slightly undercooked).
  3. Drain the rice and set aside.

3. Cooking the Mushrooms

  1. Heat oil and ghee in a large pan or pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes, green chilies, coriander, and mint leaves. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Add the marinated mushrooms and cook for 5-7 minutes on medium heat until the mushrooms are tender and coated in the spices.

4. Layering the Biryani

  1. In the same pot with the cooked mushrooms, spread a layer of partially cooked rice over the mushrooms.
  2. Drizzle the saffron milk (if using) and top with fried onions, coriander, and mint leaves.
  3. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat (dum) for 15-20 minutes, allowing the rice to steam and finish cooking.

5. Serving the Biryani

  1. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice grains.
  2. Serve hot with raita, salad, or papad.
