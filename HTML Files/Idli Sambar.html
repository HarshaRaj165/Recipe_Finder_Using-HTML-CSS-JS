Idli Sambar Recipe

Idli Sambar

Ingredients:

Ingredients for Idli

  • 2 cups idli rice or parboiled rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water for soaking and grinding
  • Salt, to taste

Ingredients for Sambar

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 small carrot, chopped
  • 1 small potato, chopped
  • 5-6 curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

Instructions for Idli

1. Soaking and Grinding

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately. Soak the rice in water for 4-6 hours, and soak the urad dal with fenugreek seeds for 4-6 hours as well.
  2. Grind the urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Grind the rice to a slightly coarse batter, adding water as needed.
  4. Mix both batters together, add salt, and let the batter ferment overnight or for 8-10 hours until it rises and becomes airy.

2. Steaming the Idlis

  1. Grease the idli molds with oil and pour the fermented batter into the molds.
  2. Steam the idlis in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without a whistle) for 10-15 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  3. Once done, remove the idlis from the molds and serve hot with sambar and chutney.

Instructions for Sambar

1. Cooking the Dal

  1. Rinse the toor dal thoroughly and cook it in a pressure cooker with water, turmeric powder, and a pinch of asafoetida for 4-5 whistles or until soft.
  2. Mash the cooked dal and set aside.

2. Cooking the Vegetables

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add curry leaves, green chilies, and chopped onions. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  3. Add chopped carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and salt. Sauté for a couple of minutes.
  4. Add sambar powder, tamarind pulp, and some water. Cover and cook until the vegetables are tender.

3. Combining the Dal and Vegetables

  1. Add the mashed dal to the cooked vegetables and mix well. Adjust the consistency with water as needed.
  2. Let the sambar simmer for 5-10 minutes to blend the flavors.

4. Serving the Sambar

  1. Garnish the sambar with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with freshly steamed idlis.
