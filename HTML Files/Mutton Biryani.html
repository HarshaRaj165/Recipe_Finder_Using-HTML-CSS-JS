Mutton Biryani Recipe

Mutton Biryani

Ingredients:

For the Mutton Marinade

  • 500g mutton (bone-in, cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala powder (or garam masala)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt, to taste

For the Rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt, to taste

For the Biryani

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

For the Mutton Marinade

  • 500g mutton (bone-in, cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala powder (or garam masala)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt, to taste

For the Rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt, to taste

For the Biryani

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Marinating the Mutton

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, biryani masala, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Mix well and marinate for at least 2-3 hours, or overnight for best results.

2. Preparing the Rice

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot. Add the bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Once the water comes to a boil, add the soaked basmati rice. Cook until the rice is about 70-80% done (slightly undercooked).
  3. Drain the rice and set aside.

3. Cooking the Mutton

  1. Heat oil and ghee in a large pan or pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes, green chilies, coriander, and mint leaves. Cook until the tomatoes soften.
  4. Add the marinated mutton and cook on medium heat until the mutton is browned and partially cooked (this may take 20-30 minutes depending on the cut of mutton).
  5. Optionally, pressure cook the mutton for 2-3 whistles to ensure it is tender.

4. Layering the Biryani

  1. In the same pot with the cooked mutton, spread a layer of partially cooked rice over the mutton.
  2. Drizzle the saffron milk (if using) and top with fried onions, coriander, and mint leaves.
  3. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes, allowing the rice to steam and finish cooking.

5. Serving the Biryani

  1. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers without breaking the rice grains.
  2. Serve hot with raita, salad, or a boiled egg on the side.
