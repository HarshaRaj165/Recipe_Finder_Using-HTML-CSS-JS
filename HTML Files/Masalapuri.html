Masalapuri Recipe

Masalapuri

Ingredients:

For Puri

  • Store-bought or homemade puris (used for pani puri)

For Masala Gravy

  • 1 cup dried white peas (soaked overnight and boiled)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

For Toppings

  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • Sev (crispy gram flour noodles)
  • Chaat masala, for sprinkling
  • Lemon juice, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Instructions:

1. Making the Puri

1. Preparing the Masala Gravy

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft.
  4. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the boiled white peas along with some water to adjust the consistency. Mash some peas with the back of a spoon to make the gravy thicker.
  6. Let the masala simmer for 5-7 minutes. Adjust salt and spices as needed. Turn off the heat and keep aside.

2. Assembling the Masala Puri

  1. Crush the puris slightly and place them on a plate as the base.
  2. Pour the prepared hot masala gravy over the crushed puris.
  3. Top with finely chopped onions, tomatoes, and sev.
  4. Sprinkle chaat masala, lemon juice, and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

3. Serving

  1. Serve the Masala Puri immediately while it's hot for the best taste.
