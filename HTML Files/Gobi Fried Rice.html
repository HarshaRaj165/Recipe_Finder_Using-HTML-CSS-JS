Gobi Fried Rice Recipe

Gobi Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked basmati rice (cooled)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup capsicum (bell peppers), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 small cauliflower (gobi), cut into small florets
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 inch ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (optional)
  • Spring onions, for garnish

Instructions:

1. Preparing the Rice

  1. Ensure the rice is cooked and completely cooled. Use leftover rice for best results, as fresh rice may turn sticky.

2. Frying the Cauliflower

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the cauliflower florets and fry until they turn golden brown and slightly crispy. Remove and set aside.

3. Stir-frying the Vegetables

  1. In the same pan, add the remaining oil and heat on high flame.
  2. Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies (if using). Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn translucent.
  4. Now add the carrots, capsicum, and green peas. Stir-fry on high heat for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crunchy.

4. Adding Seasoning

  1. Add soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper powder, and salt to taste. Stir well to combine.

5. Mixing the Rice and Cauliflower

  1. Lower the heat and add the cooked rice to the stir-fried vegetables.
  2. Gently toss the rice with the vegetables and sauces, making sure it is evenly mixed.
  3. Add the fried cauliflower and gently mix it with the rice.
  4. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes on high heat to allow the flavors to combine.

6. Serving the Gobi Fried Rice

  1. Turn off the heat and garnish the fried rice with chopped spring onions.
  2. Serve hot with a side dish of your choice or enjoy it on its own.
