Panipuri Recipe

Panipuri

Ingredients:

For Puri

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water, for kneading
  • Oil, for deep frying

For Pani (Spiced Water)

  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup coriander leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 4 cups chilled water
  • Salt, to taste

For Filling

  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas or sprouted moong beans
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • Tamarind chutney (optional)

Instructions:

1. Making the Puri

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Add water little by little and knead into a stiff dough. Cover and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  3. After resting, divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into small, thin discs (about 2 inches in diameter).
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Fry the puris until they puff up and turn golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels. Let them cool and become crisp.

2. Preparing the Pani (Spiced Water)

  1. In a blender, combine mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, and ginger. Blend into a smooth paste with a little water.
  2. Transfer the paste to a large bowl and add roasted cumin powder, black salt, chaat masala, tamarind pulp, and chilled water.
  3. Mix well and adjust the salt and spices to taste. Keep the pani chilled until serving.

3. Preparing the Filling

  1. In a bowl, mix the boiled and mashed potatoes with boiled chickpeas (or sprouted moong beans), roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, and salt.
  2. Optionally, add tamarind chutney to the mixture for extra flavor.

4. Assembling the Pani Puri

  1. Take a puri and gently crack open the top.
  2. Fill it with a small spoonful of the potato-chickpea mixture.
  3. Dip the filled puri into the spiced pani and serve immediately.
  4. Repeat with the remaining puris and enjoy your Pani Puri!
